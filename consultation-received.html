While You Wait: Resources by Stage

Regardless of where you are in your earth building journey, here are resources that might help. Take what's useful.

New to Earth Building

  • Read: "Earth Construction Handbook" by Gernot Minke
  • Watch: Search for local rammed earth buildings you can visit in person
  • Do: Make test bricks with different soil ratios—let them fail and observe why
  • Connect: Find natural building workshops in your region
  • Start: Mix earth and water with your hands for 30 minutes—just to feel the material

Some Experience, Wanting to Deepen

  • Document: Keep a detailed journal of what works and what fails—this becomes your knowledge base
  • Test: Build small test walls (2ft x 2ft) with different formwork designs and soil mixes
  • Learn: Study soil testing methods—jar test, ribbon test, drop test
  • Network: Connect with builders in the directory and ask if you can observe their projects
  • Challenge: Build something that must survive one full season exposed to weather

Experienced Builders / Professionals

  • Optimize: Document your current formwork, labor, and time costs—identify specific bottlenecks
  • Experiment: Try alternative formwork systems (modular, adjustable, lighter materials)
  • Share: Consider joining the conversation on Substack—your experience helps others
  • Collaborate: Look for other professionals in the directory who might share insights
  • Refine: Focus on the specific technical challenge you mentioned—that's where consultation is most valuable
