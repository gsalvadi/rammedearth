What Needs Strengthening

Based on your responses, here's what I recommend focusing on before pursuing tech transfer:

Three Areas to Develop

1. Direct Material Experience

The Gap: Tech transfer assumes you've already worked with earth and understand its behavior at a tactile level. If you haven't, much of the technical knowledge won't land properly.

How to Address It:

  • Build test walls or structures with rammed earth—at least 2-3 different projects
  • Work with various soil mixes and moisture levels to understand the range
  • Experience the full cycle: building, curing, weathering, and observing over time
  • Document what works, what fails, and what surprises you

Timeline: 3-6 months of hands-on experimentation

2. Technical Specificity

The Gap: Your inquiry showed interest but wasn't specific about what technical knowledge you need. Effective tech transfer requires clear understanding of your gaps.

How to Address It:

  • Identify specific technical questions from your hands-on work (not generic ones)
  • Understand which aspects of rammed earth construction you know vs. don't know
  • Develop a clear picture of what you need to learn for your practice
  • Research enough to ask informed questions

Outcome: You can articulate exactly what you need to learn and why

3. Long-Term Professional Commitment

The Gap: Tech transfer only makes sense if you're building sustained capability in your practice, not just exploring possibilities.

How to Address It:

  • Clarify if you're truly adding rammed earth as a service line or just curious
  • Consider whether you or your team will be hands-on with future projects
  • Think through how this fits your practice's direction over the next 2-3 years
  • Determine if you have organizational support for this capability development

Outcome: You have clear answers about your professional commitment level

Resources to Build Your Foundation

Essential Reading for Professionals

  • "Rammed Earth: Design and Construction Guidelines" by Ciancio & Beckett
  • "The Rammed Earth House" by David Easton (practical focus)
  • "Building with Earth" by Gernot Minke (comprehensive technical)
  • Australian Earth Building Handbook (HB 195) for modern standards

Practical Experience

  • Attend natural building workshops to get hands-on with materials
  • Visit existing rammed earth structures and study them closely
  • Build test structures on your own—start small and iterate
  • Connect with professionals in the directory for mentorship opportunities

Technical Understanding

  • Study soil science basics—particle size, plasticity, compaction
  • Learn soil testing methods you can do yourself
  • Understand structural principles specific to rammed earth
  • Research building code requirements in your jurisdiction
