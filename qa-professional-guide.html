Contributing to Community Q&A

A guide for professionals in the directory

Thank you for considering contributing your expertise!

The Community Q&A helps people who are exploring earthen building but aren't yet ready to hire someone. When you answer questions, you:

  • Demonstrate your expertise to potential clients
  • Get SEO visibility (your answer links to your directory listing)
  • Help grow the earthen building community
  • Position yourself as a knowledgeable professional
  • Create shareable content that showcases your approach

How It Works

  1. I receive questions via the site's Q&A form
  2. I review and select quality questions
  3. When a question matches your expertise/location, I'll email it to you
  4. You reply with your answer (no special format required)
  5. I publish it on the Q&A page with attribution linking to your directory listing
  6. If the asker provided their email, I notify them of the answer
No pressure: If you receive a question but don't have time or it's outside your expertise, just let me know. There's no obligation to answer every question.

What Makes a Good Answer?

1. Practical and Specific

People ask questions because they're facing real decisions. Give them actionable information.

✓ Good: "For small residential projects under 2000 sq ft, I recommend a mid-range pneumatic tamper in the 60-90 lb range. The Wacker Neuson BS60-2i works well. Make sure your air compressor can deliver minimum 15 CFM at 90 PSI. For most owner-builders, renting makes more sense than buying."
✗ Less helpful: "You need a good tamper and proper equipment. It depends on your project."

2. Share Your Experience

What have you learned from actually doing this work? Your real-world experience is more valuable than textbook knowledge.

✓ Good: "I've built cob structures in Kerala (very humid) that have performed well for 15+ years. The key is generous roof overhangs—minimum 1 meter—and allowing 6-12 months drying time before plastering."

3. Acknowledge Nuance

There's rarely one "right" answer in earthen building. Sharing trade-offs helps people make informed decisions.

✓ Good: "I prefer stabilizing with small amounts of lime (5-8%) for tropical climates. Not traditional, but improves moisture resistance. The trade-off: slightly less thermal mass, but better long-term durability in high humidity."

4. Be Honest About Limits

If something is outside your experience or requires site-specific assessment, say so.

✓ Good: "This really depends on your local soil composition and building codes. I'd need to see soil test results and know your jurisdiction to give specific guidance. In general, though..."

Answer Length

Aim for 100-300 words per answer.

  • Too short (1-2 sentences): Doesn't provide enough value
  • Just right (2-4 paragraphs): Practical, thorough, readable
  • Too long (essay): People won't read it all
Structure that works well:
  • Paragraph 1: Direct answer to the question
  • Paragraph 2: Context, caveats, or additional considerations
  • Paragraph 3: Alternative approaches or next steps (if relevant)

Topics You Might Be Asked About

Questions span all earthen building techniques:

  • Rammed earth construction
  • Cob building
  • Adobe and mud bricks
  • Compressed earth blocks (CEB)
  • Light straw clay
  • Earthbag construction
  • Professional practice (permits, codes, project management)
  • Materials and techniques
  • Climate-specific challenges
  • Troubleshooting and repairs
I'll match questions to your specific expertise and location when possible. You won't receive questions far outside your area of knowledge.

Multiple Perspectives Welcome

Sometimes multiple professionals answer the same question. This is valuable! Different approaches show there's no single "right" way, and it helps questioners understand trade-offs.

If you see a question already answered by someone else, you can still contribute if you have a different perspective or additional context.

Attribution & Visibility

Your answer will be published with attribution like this:

"— Answered by [Your Name], [Your Title], [Your Location]"

Your name will link to your directory listing, giving readers a direct path to learn more about your work and contact you.

Time Commitment

  • You might receive 1-3 questions per month (varies by specialty)
  • Each answer takes 10-20 minutes to write
  • Completely voluntary—decline any time
  • No minimum commitment

Simply reply to that email with your answer in plain text. I'll handle formatting and publishing.
